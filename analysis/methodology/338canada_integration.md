## 2. Usefulness assessment

**Verdict: less-useful for the original framing, but usable for a reframed version.**

The audit's current public report says the 2027 election sits "inside the range of plausible outcomes" for a race within 5 seats of a tie. The 338Canada snapshot on April 12, 2026 contradicts that framing directly. The model gives the UCP a >99% probability of a majority and a central seat projection 39 seats clear of the NDP. Even the lower bound of the UCP's 95% band (51 seats) is above the 44-seat majority threshold. Under 338's current aggregation, a close race is not just unlikely — it is outside the simulated distribution.

This does not invalidate the audit's 1-to-3-seat finding. It changes what the finding means for 2027 specifically. The map effect is still real and still the size that decides government formation in a close Alberta election. It just is not the size that decides 2027 if the polling stays where it is today.

**Recommendation:** replace the vague "current polling in spring 2026" sentence with an honest snapshot that acknowledges the blowout, preserves the conditional framing ("if 2027 is close"), and notes that Alberta polling has moved 10+ points inside a cycle before (see 2015 and 2023). The map effect is durable; which election it decides is contingent.

## 3. Draft paragraph for the reporter

> The 338Canada aggregator, which pools Alberta polling into a seat projection, had the UCP at 52% and the NDP at 38% as of its April 12, 2026 update, with a central projection of 63 UCP seats to 24 NDP and UCP majority odds above 99% (Fournier, 338Canada Alberta, accessed April 22, 2026). On those numbers, 2027 does not look close. That makes the audit's finding an insurance policy rather than a live intervention — a 1 to 3 seat map effect does not change a 39-seat gap. But Alberta polling has moved more than 10 points inside a single election cycle twice in the last decade, and a model aggregating April 2026 polls is not a forecast of a May 2027 result. The map is in place for two elections. Whichever of them lands close, the effect is the size that decides whether a party governs with a majority, a minority, or from opposition.

## 5. Methodology caveats to preserve

These apply whether or not the reporter integrates the draft:

1. **338Canada is a model, not a poll.** It aggregates primary polls using weighting for pollster quality, recency, and sample size. The report should say "the 338Canada aggregator" or "Fournier's model," not "polls show."
2. **2027 is approximately 13 months away as of this snapshot.** A >99% majority probability on April 12, 2026 is conditional on current polling holding — 338's probability outputs are about "if the election were held today," not "what will happen in May 2027."
3. **Alberta polling is volatile.** 2015 saw a roughly 20-point swing inside the writ period; 2023 narrowed from a projected NDP lead to a UCP majority over a few months. A 14-point April 2026 gap is not locked.
4. **The audit finding is structural, not predictive.** The 1-to-3-seat map effect is a property of the boundaries. It will sit on whatever election is next, and the one after that, regardless of what 338 shows today.
5. **If this integration stays in the report for more than ~60 days, the snapshot should be refreshed.** A stale aggregator number is worse than no number.
