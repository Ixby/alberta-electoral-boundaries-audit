Alberta Electoral Boundary Audit

An independent statistical analysis of the 2025–26 commission maps using 1,010,000 computer-simulated neutral redistributions.

Phase 4C — Official Elections Alberta shapefiles — May 2026
GitHub Public Report Academic Report One-Page Brief

What Happened

Alberta's 2025–26 Electoral Boundary Commission could not agree. Three commissioners signed a majority map; two produced a minority (alternative) map. Both were legally compliant. On April 16, 2026 the provincial government set both aside and assigned redistricting to a five-member committee of MLAs — three from the governing United Conservative Party (UCP). This audit measured both commission maps using the same statistical tools, applied identically to each.

Three Headline Numbers

Seat swing at a 50/50 vote
5 seats
At a perfectly tied provincial vote, the minority map awards 5 fewer seats to the NDP (43) than the majority map (48) — without changing a single ballot.
Vote-to-seat imbalance
41×
The minority map's structural vote-to-seat imbalance (≈36,000 excess wasted NDP votes) is roughly 41 times larger than the majority map's (≈881).
Population spread gap
48%
The minority map's average district deviates from the provincial population mean by 4,707 people — 48% wider than the majority map's 3,180.
All three differences consistently favour the UCP. The majority map's seat-allocation score falls in the normal range of the 1,010,000-map simulation (83rd percentile). The minority map's reaches the 99.99th percentile — fewer than 100 of 1,010,000 neutral draws achieve a result that extreme.

What This Does Not Claim

This audit does not conclude that either map constitutes a gerrymander in any legal sense. Canada has no statute or common-law rule that defines redistricting manipulation as a legal wrong by that name. The applicable standards are the Electoral Boundaries Commission Act (EBCA) and section 3 of the Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms, which guarantees effective representation. Whether the patterns documented here rise to a violation of either standard is a question for courts, legislators, and legal scholars — not this audit.

This audit also does not establish intent. Statistical asymmetry is consistent with deliberate manipulation, with geographic sorting of voters, or with coincidence. The audit measures effect, not motive.

The NDP geographically concentrates its voters in urban cores. Alberta's human geography creates a modest structural disadvantage for the NDP in any neutral redistricting. The simulation accounts for this: the 1,010,000-map ensemble is drawn from the same Alberta geography, and the minority map is still extreme relative to those neutral draws. The gap cannot be explained by geography alone.

Methodology at a Glance

Data

Official Elections Alberta shapefiles (GeoPackage format). Vote attribution: 2023 provincial election results matched to 4,765 voting areas by centroid-in-polygon spatial join (Phase 4C). Population: Statistics Canada 2021 Census dissemination areas.

Simulation

1,010,000 neutral Alberta maps generated by the ReCom Markov-chain algorithm (GerryChain library), four independent chains, using the same population-equality rules that governed the commission. Gelman-Rubin convergence statistic R̂ < 1.02 across all chains. Random seeds derived from Cloudflare drand public randomness beacon.

Pre-registration

All statistical tests were pre-registered on the Open Science Framework before results were examined: OSF:6pt83, AsPredicted:#289,469, AsPredicted:#289,451.
