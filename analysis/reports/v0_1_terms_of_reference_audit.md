# Terms of Reference Audit — Alberta 2025-26 EBC

Cross-check of every TOR-dependent claim in `report_academic.md` and `report_public.md` against the commission's own statement of its mandate (`v0_1_terms_of_reference_verbatim.md`) and the verbatim text of the **Electoral Boundaries Commission Act, RSA 2000 c. E-3** (office consolidation current as of Dec 5, 2024, retrieved from Alberta King's Printer PDF, reference: `kings-printer.alberta.ca/documents/Acts/E03.pdf`).

**Source documents used.**
- Commission final report, pp. 1, 13–16, 27–33, 58–67 — `alberta_audit/.temp/commission_report.pdf`
- Electoral Boundaries Commission Act, RSA 2000 c. E-3 — King's Printer office consolidation, 2024 Dec 5, 12 pp. (Act text quoted verbatim below.)
- CanLII metadata: `https://www.canlii.org/en/ab/laws/stat/rsa-2000-c-e-3/latest/rsa-2000-c-e-3.html`
- Audit reports under review: `alberta_audit/report_academic.md` and `alberta_audit/report_public.md`

**Operational convention.** "CONFIRMED" = audit claim matches statute and/or commission TOR; "NEEDS QUALIFICATION" = essentially correct but imprecise wording; "DISCREPANCY" = material mismatch that will affect a downstream finding. Load-bearing discrepancies (ones that move a headline finding) are in **bold**.

## 1. Verbatim Act text (reference)

From the King's Printer consolidation of Dec 5, 2024:

**s. 2(1) — Composition:** "an Electoral Boundaries Commission is to be appointed consisting of (a) a chair appointed by the Lieutenant Governor in Council, who must be one of the following: (i) the Ethics Commissioner; (ii) the Auditor General; (iii) the president of a post-secondary educational institution in Alberta; (iv) a judge or retired judge of any court in Alberta; (v) a person whose stature and qualifications are, in the opinion of the Lieutenant Governor in Council, similar to those of the persons referred to in subclauses (i) to (iv), (b) 2 persons, who are not members of the Legislative Assembly, appointed by the Speaker of the Legislative Assembly on the nomination of the Leader of His Majesty's loyal opposition in consultation with the leaders of the other opposition parties represented in the Legislative Assembly, and (c) 2 persons, who are not members of the Legislative Assembly, appointed by the Speaker of the Legislative Assembly on the nomination of the President of the Executive Council."

**s. 2(2) — CEO support:** "The Chief Electoral Officer is to provide advice, information and assistance to the Commission."

**s. 5 — Time of appointment:** "5(1) A Commission is to be appointed on or before October 31, 2016. (2) Subsequent Commissions are to be appointed during the first session of the Legislature following every 2nd general election after the appointment of the last Commission. (3) Notwithstanding subsection (2), if less than 8 years has elapsed since the appointment of the last Commission, the Commission is to be appointed (a) no sooner than 8 years, and (b) no later than 10 years after the appointment of the last Commission."

**s. 6(1) — Interim report timing:** "The Commission shall, after considering any representations to it and within 7 months of the date on which the Commission is appointed, submit to the Speaker of the Legislative Assembly a report..."

**s. 7(1) — Public hearings:** "The Commission must hold public hearings both (a) before its report is submitted to the Speaker, and (b) after its report has been made public..."

**s. 8(1) — Final report timing:** "The Commission may, after considering any further representations made to it and within 5 months of the date it submitted its report, submit to the Speaker a final report."

**s. 9 — Majority/chair rule:** "If there is more than one report submitted under section 6 or 8, the report of a majority of the members of the Commission is the report of the Commission, but if there is no majority, the report of the chair is the report of the Commission."

**s. 12 — Population basis:** (full verbatim in `v0_1_terms_of_reference_verbatim.md` §4). Subsections (3), (4), (5) control: decennial is the default; province-wide census displaces decennial if more recent; "more recent information respecting the population of all or any part of Alberta" may be used "in conjunction with" the §12(3) or §12(4) base.

**s. 13 — Number of divisions:** "13 The Commission shall divide Alberta into 89 proposed electoral divisions. RSA 2000 cE-3 s13; 2009 c19 s4; **2024 c17 s3**." (The 2024 amendment raised the number from 87 to 89.)

**s. 14 — Criteria:** (full verbatim in §3b of the verbatim file) — effective representation mandatory; six permissive factors (a)–(f).

**s. 15(1) — ±25% variance:** "The population of a proposed electoral division must not be more than 25% above nor more than 25% below the average population of all the proposed electoral divisions."

**s. 15(2) — Special remote district exception (the five criteria):** "(2) Notwithstanding subsection (1), in the case of no more than 4 of the proposed electoral divisions, if the Commission is of the opinion that at least 3 of the following criteria exist in a proposed electoral division, the proposed electoral division may have a population that is as much as 50% below the average population of all the proposed electoral divisions:
- (a) the area of the proposed electoral division exceeds **20 000 square kilometres** or the total surveyed area exceeds **15 000 square kilometres**;
- (b) the distance from the **Legislature Building in Edmonton** to the nearest boundary of the proposed electoral division by the most direct highway route is **more than 150 kilometres**;
- (c) there is **no town** in the proposed electoral division that has **a population exceeding 8000 people**;
- (d) the area of the proposed electoral division contains **an Indian reserve or a Metis settlement**;
- (e) the proposed electoral division has a portion of its boundary **coterminous with a boundary of the Province of Alberta**."

**s. 15(3):** "For the purpose of subsection (2)(c), The Municipality of Crowsnest Pass is not a town."

Note: the statute does **not** use "exceptional electoral district" or "special remote district" as named labels. Those are descriptive terms of art.

## 2. Claim-by-claim verification

### 2.1 Composition

| # | Audit claim | TOR / Act text | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| 6 | `report_public.md` line 75: "Two of its five members are government-appointed, two are opposition-appointed, and the chair is named by the Chief Justice." | **Act s. 2(1)(a):** Chair is "appointed by the Lieutenant Governor in Council" from a pool of five designated classes of office-holder including (iv) "a judge or retired judge of any court in Alberta." The Chief Justice has no appointment role in the Act. The commission report p. 13 confirms: "The Honourable Justice Dallas K. Miller was appointed by the **Lieutenant Governor in Council** as Chairman." Two opposition-nominated members are appointed by the **Speaker** on nomination by the Leader of the Opposition; two government-nominated members are appointed by the **Speaker** on nomination by the President of the Executive Council. | **DISCREPANCY** |

**What the audit should change.** Replace "the chair is named by the Chief Justice" with "the chair is appointed by the Lieutenant Governor in Council and must be drawn from a statutory list that includes the Ethics Commissioner, the Auditor General, a post-secondary institution president, or a sitting or retired judge." Also clarify that the four non-chair commissioners are appointed by the Speaker of the Legislative Assembly on party-leader nominations (not directly by the parties). This is factual on statute and on the commission's own p. 13 language.

**Load-bearing?** Moderate. The audit's "independent commission" framing (PART I, PART II "The commission split" section) rests on judicial independence of the chair; replacing "Chief Justice" with the correct "Lieutenant Governor in Council from a judicial class" does not weaken the independence claim — a sitting judge was in fact appointed — but it does require correcting the mechanism. The audit also elsewhere (academic report Abstract, §1.4) refers generically to "an independent commission" without misattributing the appointment; those passages are fine.

### 2.4 Section 15(2) five-criteria test — **multiple threshold errors**

| # | Audit claim | Act text | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5a | `report_academic.md` §2.4 p. 186: "(a) area > 20,000 km²" | **Act s. 15(2)(a):** area exceeds **20 000 square kilometres** OR total surveyed area exceeds **15 000 square kilometres**. The audit cites only the 20 000 km² limb and misses the alternative 15 000 km² surveyed-area limb. | **DISCREPANCY** |
| 5b | `report_academic.md` §2.4 p. 186: "(b) > 100 km from a major centre" | **Act s. 15(2)(b):** "the distance from **the Legislature Building in Edmonton** to the nearest boundary of the proposed electoral division by the most direct highway route is **more than 150 kilometres**." The audit's "100 km from a major centre" is wrong on two counts: (i) threshold is **150 km, not 100 km**; (ii) the distance is measured from the **Legislature Building in Edmonton specifically**, not from any "major centre." | **DISCREPANCY** |
| 5c | `report_academic.md` §2.4 p. 186: "(c) no town with 4,000+ population in the district" | **Act s. 15(2)(c):** "there is no town in the proposed electoral division that has a population exceeding **8000 people**." The audit's "4,000+" threshold is **wrong by half**. s. 15(3): "The Municipality of Crowsnest Pass is not a town." | **DISCREPANCY** |
| 5d | "(d) significant Indigenous population or reserves" | **Act s. 15(2)(d):** "the area of the proposed electoral division contains **an Indian reserve or a Metis settlement**." The audit's "significant Indigenous population" loosens the test — the statute is a presence/absence test for **a** reserve or settlement, not a demographic threshold. | NEEDS QUALIFICATION |
| 5e | "(e) shared border with another province or the US" | **Act s. 15(2)(e):** "has a portion of its boundary coterminous with a boundary of the Province of Alberta." The audit's "another province or the US" is materially correct — Alberta's boundary is with BC, Saskatchewan, Northwest Territories, and Montana (US) — but this is not in the statute explicitly. The statute simply says "coterminous with a boundary of the Province of Alberta." | NEEDS QUALIFICATION |

**Downstream impact of the 5a/5b/5c threshold errors** is substantial:

- **Canmore-Banff (majority s.15(2))**, audit says 1/5 criteria pass. Corrected check:
  - (a) area: Canmore-Banff spans Banff National Park and parts of Kananaskis — the audit's "well below 20,000 km²" call may still stand, but would need re-checking against the alternative 15,000 km² surveyed-area test.
  - (b) distance from Legislature Building: Canmore is ~400 km from the Legislature via Highway 2 / 1; the nearest boundary of the ED would be substantially more than 150 km. **(b) is PASS**, not "borderline" as the academic §2.4 note says.
  - (c) "no town exceeding 8000 people": Banff townsite is ~8,000; Canmore is ~15,000. Canmore alone defeats (c). **Audit call stands**, but the audit's reasoning "two towns over 4,000" uses the wrong threshold. The correct statement is that Canmore alone exceeds 8,000, so (c) fails.
  - (d) Indian reserve or Metis settlement: the audit says "limited Indigenous population within boundary." The correct test is whether the boundary *contains* a reserve or Metis settlement. Stoney Nakoda Nation (Morley Reserve) lies in the Canmore-Banff / Banff-Kananaskis area — this should be re-verified against the majority's boundary map, but the raw statutory test is a geographic-presence test and may **flip from "fails" to "passes"**.
  - (e) "coterminous with Alberta boundary": Canmore-Banff extends to the BC border. **(e) PASSES.**
  - Corrected count: likely 2–3 of 5, not 1 of 5. If (d) passes (which depends on whether the Morley Reserve is inside the final boundary), Canmore-Banff may meet the 3/5 threshold legitimately, which would **materially change the §2.4 finding**.

- **Rocky Mountain House-Banff Park (minority s.15(2))**, audit says 2/5 criteria pass. Corrected check:
  - (a) area: audit says "~22,000 km² area criterion is met only through extension of the boundary through the uninhabited portion of Banff National Park." If the statute's 15 000 km² surveyed-area alternative is considered, the engineered-extension argument needs to be re-run against that alternative. The audit's current engineering claim relies on the 20 000 km² limb; with the 15 000 km² alternative a smaller non-park boundary might still pass on area.
  - (b) 150 km from Legislature Building: the nearest boundary of Rocky Mountain House-Banff Park (Rocky Mountain House is ~220 km southwest of Edmonton) is well more than 150 km. **(b) PASSES** — this was not in the audit's 2/5 count and adds a criterion.
  - (c) no town > 8,000: Rocky Mountain House town population ~7,100 (2021 Census). Sundre is ~2,700. Clearwater County is rural. No town inside the ED exceeds 8,000. **(c) PASSES** — this was also not in the audit's 2/5 count.
  - (d) Indian reserve or Metis settlement: ED may contain Sunchild First Nation or O'Chiese reserves — needs map verification.
  - (e) coterminous with Alberta boundary: depends on the minority's NP extension; audit says this only passes because of the extension.
  - Corrected count: the minority's boundary **may already pass 3/5 without the NP extension** via (b), (c), and possibly (d). The audit's core claim that the NP extension is "engineered" to meet the 3/5 test depends on at least two of the criteria the audit miscounted. **This is load-bearing and the §2.4 finding needs to be re-run.**

**What the audit should change.** §2.4 of `report_academic.md` needs a full re-audit against the correct statutory thresholds. The engineered-boundary claim for RMH-Banff Park may still stand once the re-audit is done, but the argument path changes. At minimum the audit must correct the threshold values wherever they appear and flag the §2.4 and §C3 findings as contingent on a re-audit.

**Load-bearing?** **Yes, substantially.** §2.4 is cited in the public report lines 151–152 ("Rocky Mountain House-Banff Park … extending through uninhabited national park land … One extra rural seat"), in the §5 rationale-failure count, and in the C1/C3 checklist. The seat-count allocation of ~0.7 of the 1-to-3 minority-vs-majority gap is pegged to this one redraw (public report Table 4, line 151).

### 2.5 Population basis — decennial vs 2024 estimate

| # | Audit claim | Act / TOR text | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Audit claim: the commission was required to use 2021 census data as the "legal baseline" | **Act s. 12(3):** "Subject to subsections (4) and (5), the Commission is to use (a) the population information as provided in the decennial census." s. 12(4) permits substitution if there is a *province-wide census* more recent than the decennial. s. 12(5): "The Commission may, as it considers appropriate, use more recent information respecting the population of all or any part of Alberta **in conjunction with** the information referred to in subsection (3) or (4)." | NEEDS QUALIFICATION |
| 2 | The commission used 2024 Treasury Board estimates instead | Commission p. 29: "This Commission was unanimous in agreeing to use the 2021 decennial census (updated to a July 1, 2024, estimate by the Alberta Treasury Board's Office of Statistics and Information) as the population of Alberta." **What the audit should change.** `report_academic.md` already handles this correctly in its "Data-basis preamble" (§2.1) and §9 "Scope and limitations", where the audit explicitly flags: (a) the "operative force of §12(3)" is an unresolved statutory-interpretation question; (b) all §A verdicts are stable whether computed on the 2021 census, 2024 OSI estimate, or 2025 TBF estimate (Plan B cross-check). **Small sharpening.** The audit's phrase "the 2021 decennial Statistics Canada census, the data source the Electoral Boundaries Commission Act §12(3) designates as the Commission's mandatory basis" (§9) overstates slightly. §12(3) is **subject to** §12(4) and §12(5), so the "mandatory basis" language should be "default basis absent §12(4)/(5) supplementation." This is a drafting tweak, not a substantive finding change.

### 2.6 Statutory criteria — community of interest, contiguity, compactness

| # | Audit claim | Act text | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| 11 | "Cities and counties should stay intact where possible. Rivers, highways, and municipal limits make better boundaries. Communities that share services ... belong together." (`report_public.md` line 59) | **Act s. 14:** effective representation is mandatory; six permissive considerations follow — (a) sparsity/density/growth rate, (b) communities of interest "including municipalities, regional and rural communities, Indian reserves and Metis settlements," (c) geographical features, (d) availability and means of communication and transportation, (e) desirability of understandable and clear boundaries, (f) any other factors. **The word "compactness" does not appear in the statute**, nor does "contiguity," nor "counties" as a specific unit. "Municipalities" is explicit. | NEEDS QUALIFICATION |

**What the audit should change.** Replace "Cities and counties should stay intact" with "the Act directs the commission to consider 'communities of interest, including municipalities, regional and rural communities, Indian reserves and Metis settlements.'" The phrase "counties" is not in the statute; "regional and rural communities" is. Contiguity is not a statutory criterion in Alberta — the audit implicitly assumes it without citing it. Compactness is not a statutory criterion. The public report's plain-language paraphrase is a reasonable restatement of the statutory factors but should not be presented as if it were the statute's own language.

**Load-bearing?** Low. The plain-language framing in the public report is a reader-accessibility choice, not a technical claim.

## 4. Open questions (items I could not verify in the budget)

1. **Order in Council for the 2025 commission's appointment.** The commission report names the Lieutenant Governor in Council as the appointing authority for the chair and the Speaker as the appointing authority for the four non-chair members, but does not cite a specific OC or appointment-letter number for either. No specific OC number needed to confirm any current audit claim, but flagged for completeness.

2. **Geographic verification of s. 15(2) criterion passes.** Corrected-threshold re-audits of Canmore-Banff and Rocky Mountain House-Banff Park require cross-checking each ED's boundary against: (i) distance-from-Legislature measurements along shortest highway routes (may require GIS on the 2026 boundary files, which are not yet published); (ii) presence of specific Indian reserves and Metis settlements inside the boundary; (iii) ED-area totals against the 20,000 km² and 15,000 km² alternatives. Without the 2026 shapefiles (noted blocked throughout the audit), this is a bounded re-audit that can be done against the commission's published maps and rough area claims in the commission report.

3. **2024 Alberta Treasury Board OSI estimate as "province-wide census" vs "more recent information."** The commission characterizes the 4,888,723 figure as "decennial census updated to July 1, 2024 estimate" (p. 29) — i.e., a §12(5) "more recent information" supplement to §12(3), not a §12(4) province-wide census displacement. The audit's §9 already flags this interpretation question. No new question raised by the TOR audit.

4. **Precise Alberta city and town populations for s. 15(2)(c) threshold application.** At the 8,000 threshold, several Alberta towns sit near the line (e.g., Banff townsite, Whitecourt, Olds). The audit's original 4,000 threshold meant many more towns qualified as disqualifying. With the correct 8,000 threshold, several previously-disqualifying towns may no longer disqualify, changing the arithmetic.

## 5. Net assessment of the audit

**Load-bearing findings that survive TOR cross-check:**
- Chair-only attribution of Recommendation 5 (§2.9 — CONFIRMED)
- Commission mandate was 89 seats, not 91; the 91-seat path requires a legislative amendment (§2.2 — CONFIRMED)
- Decennial + Treasury-Board supplementation framework characterization (§2.5 — CONFIRMED with a minor drafting note)
- ±25% statutory variance band (§2.3 — CONFIRMED)
- Mandatory public hearings and mandatory submission-consideration (§2.7 — CONFIRMED)
- Timeline (§2.8 — CONFIRMED)

**Load-bearing findings that need re-work against corrected thresholds:**
- §2.4 s. 15(2) eligibility verdicts for Canmore-Banff and Rocky Mountain House-Banff Park — **re-audit required**
- §C checklist "engineered-boundary" signature for RMH-Banff Park — depends on §2.4 re-audit outcome
- §5 per-redraw seat count — the ~0.7 seat allocated to RMH-Banff Park s. 15(2) invocation is contingent on that redraw actually being an engineered s. 15(2) pass rather than a legitimate pass

**Non-load-bearing discrepancies:**
- Commission composition mechanism (Chief Justice vs. Lieutenant Governor in Council) — factual correction, does not undermine any current audit claim
- Plain-language paraphrase of s. 14 in `report_public.md` — reader-accessibility tweak
