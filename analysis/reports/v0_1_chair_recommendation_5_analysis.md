# Chair Recommendation 5 analysis

**Date:** 2026-04-22
**Claim under test:** Premier Danielle Smith stated that the April 16, 2026 motion replacing the commission's process with an MLA-led committee "aligns with a recommendation from the commission's chair, who had suggested creating more seats in the legislature to prevent eliminating two rural ridings" (Calgary Journal, April 21, 2026).
**Verdict:** **TRUE in form, CONDITIONAL in substance, INVERTED in intent.**

---

## The chair's actual text

Chair Dallas K. Miller, Addendum to the Majority Report, commission final report pp. 66–67, signed "The Honourable Dallas K. Miller, Chair":

> "Recommendation 5: In the circumstance that the Legislative Assembly of Alberta finds that it cannot concur in the proposals of the majority of the Electoral Boundaries Commission in its final report to remove two electoral divisions from areas outside of the Cities of Edmonton and Calgary, the majority of the Commission recommends that the Assembly make amendments to the Electoral Divisions Act, increasing the number of electoral divisions from 89 to 91 for the next general election, and restore the two electoral divisions that this Commission has removed. The rest of the province as we propose must be maintained to the extent possible.
>
> This would need to be studied by an all-party Select Special Committee or other equivalent Legislative Committee in accordance with its Standing Orders. However, restoration of the two electoral divisions should:
>
> a) have no impact on any electoral division in Airdrie or containing territory south of Airdrie's southernmost border, with the exception of Drumheller-Stettler;
> b) have no impact on any electoral division north of the North Saskatchewan River in Edmonton;
> c) result in all electoral divisions south of the North Saskatchewan River in Edmonton reverting to the map outlined in the interim report, with the possible exception of including Nisku in Edmonton-South East if the all-party committee wishes to restore Leduc and Beaumont being in the same electoral division; and
> d) consider restoring an electoral division mostly consisting of Clearwater County and western Mountain View County as an electoral division with s. 15(2) status under the Act."

Chair's stated purpose of Recommendation 5 (p. 66, same Addendum):

> "This fifth recommendation is formulated for the express purpose of dissuading the Legislature from accepting the minority report."

Chair's own framing of the problem (p. 66):

> "The problem this Commission faced, in my view, is not the statutory criteria for drawing electoral districts. Rather, it is the fact that the size of the legislature was increased by only 2%, while the population increased by 20%."

**Chair's admission in R5 that the majority did not agree with him (p. 66):**

> "My majority colleagues do not agree with me on this point."

This sentence is significant for the April 16 provenance framing. R5's opening is drafted in the voice of "the majority of the Commission," but Miller explicitly disavows that voice in the same document. The recommendation is Miller's personal addendum, not a collective majority proposal.

**Corroboration from Commissioner Greg Clark (social-media, April 2026).** Commissioner Greg Clark, one of the two opposition-nominated majority commissioners (nominated by NDP Leader Naheed Nenshi), posted a thread on X / social media after the final report's tabling in April 2026 clarifying that the 91-seat call came from the chair alone, not from the majority commissioners. Clark's thread was referenced by multiple outlets (rabble.ca, albertapolitics.substack.com). Full citation pending direct archival retrieval at @GregClarkAB; the substance is already established by Miller's own in-text admission above.

**Corroborating news report.** CBC News Edmonton, April 16, 2026 ("MLA committee to review Alberta electoral boundaries after report is set aside") quotes Miller's "My majority colleagues do not agree with me on this point" and summarises: *"Miller's 91-seat recommendation was conditional on the legislature not accepting the majority report. It was not supported by the other majority members of the commission."* URL: `https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/edmonton/alberta-boundaries-committee-motion-9.7172743`.

## The three-layer finding

1. **Form:** The vehicle the government chose (a Select Special Committee raising seats from 89 to 91 to preserve two rural divisions) is the vehicle Chair Miller explicitly proposed. On form, Premier Smith's claim of alignment with the chair's recommendation is accurate.

2. **Conditions:** R5 specified four substantive boundary constraints (a–d) plus the framing that "the rest of the province as we propose must be maintained to the extent possible." Whether the government's process respects these constraints is testable only after the committee tables its map in November 2026. If the final 91-seat map violates any of (a–d), or introduces hybrid configurations from the minority report that the chair classified as unreasonable (Airdrie, Calgary, Chestermere, Cochrane, Red Deer, St. Albert), the alignment with R5 fails on substance.

3. **Intent:** Chair Miller stated plainly that R5's purpose was to dissuade the Legislature from accepting the minority report. The chair's Addendum specifically described the minority's hybrids in Airdrie, Calgary, Chestermere, Cochrane, Red Deer, and St. Albert as "not something that I can condone" and said the minority report was "substantively unreasonable" and "likely to offend s. 3 of the Charter." A committee whose output reintroduces those minority configurations invokes the form of R5 while inverting its intent.

## What this changes in the audit's framing

**The current report_public.md framing** ("Replacing an independent boundary commission with a government-majority committee of MLAs is the thing the independent-commission model exists to prevent. Quebec's government in 1992, Ontario's in 1996, and BC's in 2008 all amended commission recommendations after the fact. None of them replaced the commission itself with a legislative committee.") is directionally correct but requires one qualification: **the specific mechanism the government used was sanctioned in advance by the commission's own chair.** This does not neutralize the concern; it reframes it.

**The revised framing** should distinguish three things:
- The April 16 vehicle (Select Special Committee raising seats to 91) is chair-sanctioned as a fallback.
- The April 16 mandate (whether the committee's output respects R5 conditions a–d and the chair's "rest of province as we propose" constraint) is **not yet testable** — it is what the November checklist should watch for.
- The April 16 political framing (that the UCP is "following the chair's recommendation") is accurate as to vehicle, insufficient as to constraints, and inverted if the final map adopts the minority's flagged hybrids.

## Proposed insertion — report_public.md

Target section: "The 91-seat proposal, so far" (near current April 16 paragraph) — add one short paragraph.

> The government has framed its April 16 decision as following a fallback recommendation by the commission's chair. Chair Dallas Miller did write, in an Addendum to the majority report, that if the Legislature could not accept the majority's 89-seat boundaries, it should raise the seat count to 91 through an all-party Select Special Committee and restore the two rural divisions the majority report removed. That recommendation existed, and the government's chosen vehicle matches it. But the chair also attached four specific boundary conditions to his Recommendation 5, required the "rest of the province as we propose" to be maintained, and stated plainly that the recommendation's purpose was to dissuade the Legislature from accepting the minority report. The committee's eventual map will answer whether April 16 used the chair's mechanism for the chair's purpose, or for a purpose the chair explicitly wrote against.

## Proposed insertion — report_academic.md

Target section: §Procedural — subsection on the April 16 action.

> Premier Smith's public framing of the April 16 motion as aligned with a recommendation by Chair Miller is accurate as to the procedural vehicle: Recommendation 5 in the Chair's Addendum to the Majority Report (commission final report, pp. 66–67) proposed an amendment to the Electoral Divisions Act raising the seat count from 89 to 91, to be studied by "an all-party Select Special Committee or other equivalent Legislative Committee." The alignment is partial on three grounds. First, R5 specifies four boundary conditions (a–d, at p. 66) that constrain the restoration of the two removed rural divisions, including no impact on Airdrie or territory south of it (with a single exception for Drumheller-Stettler), no impact north of Edmonton's North Saskatchewan River, and a reversion to the interim-report map for south-of-NSR Edmonton. Whether the committee's November output respects those conditions is not yet testable. Second, R5 requires the rest of the province to be maintained "to the extent possible" on the majority's 89-seat map, not the minority's. Third, and most material, the Chair stated that R5 "is formulated for the express purpose of dissuading the Legislature from accepting the minority report" and that the minority's hybrid configurations in Airdrie, Calgary, Chestermere, Cochrane, Red Deer, and St. Albert were "not something that I can condone." A committee output that reintroduces any of those configurations invokes the form of R5 while inverting its intent. The November audit should therefore test the map against R5(a)–(d) as a pre-registered gate before evaluating partisan-math findings.
