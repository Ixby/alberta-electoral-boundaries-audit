# v0.1 Minority and Government Rationales — Structured Inventory

**Scope.** Every rationale offered by (a) the two dissenting minority commissioners in Appendix E of the 2025–26 EBC final report, (b) Premier Smith and UCP ministers, (c) UCP MLAs including committee chair Brandon Lunty, and (d) the April 16, 2026 motion itself, for either the contested minority-map configurations or the decision to set aside the commission's majority report.

**Primary-source base.**
- Commission final report, Appendix E (pp. 285–362). Text extracted in `.temp/appendix_e_text.txt`; this file quotes verbatim with page references.
- Alberta Legislative Assembly, April 16, 2026 motion and debate (reported by CBC Edmonton, Calgary Journal, Black Press chain).
- Premier's April 16 press remarks (reported CBC, Rimbey Review, Times Colonist, etc.).
- Brandon Lunty public statements (Rimbey Review chain, April 16).
- Canada's National Observer April 17 report.

**Key identification.** The two minority commissioners are **Dr. Julian Martin (Sherwood Park)** and **John D. Evans (Lethbridge)**, both appointed by Premier Smith. Majority chair is **Justice Dallas K. Miller**; the other two majority commissioners were NDP-nominated.

**Closed tests (do not revisit).** Five population-math tests in `v0_1_justification_tests_findings.md` — all FAIL. Those five closed justifications are noted `CLOSED-FAIL` and excluded from Phase 2 re-testing.

## Inventory Table — Minority Commissioners (Appendix E)

### R1. Calgary-Nolan Hill-Cochrane hybrid

- **Source:** Minority commissioners (Martin/Evans)
- **Venue:** EBC final report, Appendix E, p. 331 (text extraction line 1658–1676)
- **Verbatim:** *"This configuration responds to sustained growth in northwest Calgary and along the Highway 1A and Highway 22 corridors, while recognizing the established social and economic integration between these communities and the Town of Cochrane. The proposed division links the northwest Calgary neighbourhoods of Nolan Hill, Sage Hill, and surrounding developing areas with Cochrane and adjacent lands that share transportation, employment, recreation, and service networks… residents move fluidly between jurisdictions for work, education, health care, and commerce."*
- **District defended:** Calgary-Nolan Hill-Cochrane
- **Type:** Commuter-tie + Growth-projection + Community-of-interest

### R2. Calgary-Peigan-Chestermere (east Calgary + south Chestermere)

- **Source:** Minority (Martin/Evans)
- **Venue:** Appendix E, p. 332 (lines 1712–1725)
- **Verbatim:** *"Erin Woods is included in Calgary-Peigan-Chestermere, and the boundary is extended beyond the city to incorporate the southern portions of Chestermere. These changes reflect the growing social, economic, and transportation connections between east Calgary and Chestermere."*
- **District defended:** Calgary-Peigan-Chestermere
- **Type:** Commuter-tie + Growth-projection + Community-of-interest

### R3. Calgary-Airdrie (new hybrid, north Calgary + south Airdrie)

### R12. Rocky Mountain House-Banff Park (s.15(2) exception)

- **Source:** Minority
- **Venue:** Appendix E, p. 352 (lines 2457–2477)
- **Verbatim:** *"We propose this electoral district receive an exemption under section 15(2)… geographic size, location on a provincial border, and small communities contained within this electoral division makes it an appropriate use for the exemption… the north-south character of economic corridors in the region along Highway 22; the unique nature of Rocky Mountain House being the only town in Clearwater County and acting as a hub for the entire surrounding population; the implications of dividing regional Indian reserves from the nearest economic hub; and the historical precedent of portions of Banff National Park being included in a west central Alberta electoral division."*
- **District defended:** Rocky Mountain House-Banff Park
- **Type:** Area (s.15(2)(a)) + Economic-zone + Indigenous-consultation + Historical-continuity
- **Note:** Population-math (s.15(2)(a) area argument) already tested — CLOSED-FAIL (v0_1_justification_tests, Test 2). Remaining untested claims: Indigenous-consultation, Historical-continuity, Economic-zone.

### R13. Olds-Three Hills-Didsbury (with Rocky View extension)

The revised boundaries keep established communities of interest together along the Highway 2 corridor between Red Deer and Airdrie, including Olds, Carstairs, and Crossfield… The electoral division maintains Highway 22 as a western boundary, responding to feedback from the public about the fundamental distinction between communities of interest and effective representation on either side of that transportation corridor."*

### R17. Hybrid-constituency doctrine (general rationale)

- **Source:** Minority
- **Venue:** Appendix E, pp. 302–303 (lines 542–584)
- **Verbatim:** *"Hybrid constituencies are not only a good reflection of Alberta, but also the best available instrument for preserving rural and suburban representation in the face of sustained urban growth. They avoid the false choice between (a) underrepresenting Calgary and Edmonton, and (b) creating ever-larger purely rural districts that become geographically unmanageable… the amended legislation does not merely permit hybrid divisions in Calgary and Edmonton; it equips the Commission to respond to metropolitan growth in a way that aligns electoral boundaries with the functional realities of Alberta's two largest regional economies."*
- **District defended:** General hybrid doctrine (applies to all Calgary, Red Deer, Lethbridge hybrids)
- **Type:** Rural-preservation + Growth-projection + Economic-zone

### R18. Projected-growth rationale (blanket)

- **Source:** Minority
- **Venue:** Appendix E, p. 306 (lines 720–723)
- **Verbatim:** *"The Commission considered anticipated population growth patterns, recognizing that growth rates vary significantly across regions. It is difficult to be confident about anticipated growth in any given area, especially the timing and the intensity."*
- **District defended:** Calgary-Airdrie, Calgary-Nolan Hill-Cochrane, Calgary-Bow-Springbank, Chestermere-Strathmore
- **Type:** Growth-projection
- **Note:** Self-hedged; minority concedes uncertainty in own text.

## Inventory Table — April 16 Motion Text

### R24. Motion terms — MLA committee + 91 seats, no public hearings

- **Source:** Government motion, Alberta Legislative Assembly (introduced Government House Leader Joseph Schow)
- **Venue:** Order Paper April 16, 2026; passed 44–36 (party-line)
- **Verbatim / operative terms (reported):** Motion rejects the commission's proposed boundaries; creates a Special Select Committee on Electoral Boundaries chaired by Brandon Lunty (3 UCP, 2 NDP); committee receives advice from an independent advisory panel that examines 91-seat configuration; committee will NOT hold public hearings on any new map; report due November 2, 2026.
- **Procedural escape clauses noted (Daveberta Substack citation of motion text):** advisory panel conducts review *"despite any vacancies in its membership"*; if multiple reports exist, *"the report of a majority of the members of the independent advisory panel is the report."*
- **Defends:** Itself — the set-aside decision
- **Type:** Procedural

### R25. Motion — 91-seat ceiling tied to 20% provincial growth

- **Source:** Government (motion rationale as publicly framed)
- **Venue:** April 16, 2026
- **Verbatim paraphrase:** the government could increase seats to 91 to reflect Alberta's 20% population increase since the 2017 boundaries commission report.
- **Defends:** 91 vs. 89 choice
- **Type:** Growth-projection + Procedural
