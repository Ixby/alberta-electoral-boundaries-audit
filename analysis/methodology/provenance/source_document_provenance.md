# v0.1 Commission Source Provenance — Track O

**Purpose.** Trace the provenance of every population figure the 2025-2026
Alberta Electoral Boundaries Commission published, verify that the cited
sources actually say what the commission claims, identify any
inconsistencies, and recommend disclosure language for a future Act §12
amendment that would close the gaps this trace uncovers.

The audit's headline finding on data sources already exists in
`analysis/reports/plan_b_cross_check.md`: the commission's stated basis
(2021 decennial census updated to July 1, 2024) is inconsistent with the
actual tables, which derive directly from the July 2024 Alberta Treasury
Board OSI estimate without a visible 2021 census component. This document
goes a level deeper: it verifies each cited source against its public
release, locates the 50-person arithmetic gap in the minority table, and
produces an inconsistency table with page citations precise enough to
survive cross-examination.

- Neither report cites any specific Statistics Canada table by catalogue
  number (17-10-0009 for quarterly provincial estimates, 98-10-0005 for

- Neither report cites the TBF "Annual Population Report, Alberta
  2023–2024" (December 10, 2024) or the "Quarterly Population Report
  Second Quarter 2024" by name, even though both publications contain the
  4,888,723 figure.
- Neither report states whether the per-ED figures were produced by the

## Phase 2 — Source verification

### 2.1 Does StatsCan Table 17-10-0009 (quarterly provincial estimates) produce 4,888,723 for Alberta on July 1, 2024?

**Yes.** The StatsCan Daily release for Q2 2024 ("Canada's population
estimates, second quarter 2024," released September 25, 2024) reports
Alberta's July 1, 2024 population at **4,888,723**. This is the exact
figure the commission uses as its provincial total.

Source verified: https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/daily-quotidien/240925/dq240925d-eng.htm

### 2.2 Does the TBF July 2024 estimate produce 4,888,723?

**Yes.** The Alberta TBF "Annual Population Report, Alberta 2023–2024"
(OSI, released December 10, 2024) reports Alberta's mid-year 2024
population at **4,888,723**, an increase of 204,209 (4.4%) over the
previous twelve-month period.

Source verified: `open.alberta.ca/dataset/1050cf0a-8c1d-4875-9800-b7d2f3199e41/resource/b622c22b-507c-4eb2-9c7b-519e2cb75be1/download/tbf-annual-population-report-alberta-2023-2024.pdf`

### 2.3 What does the commission's provincial total actually come from?

Both sources agree at 4,888,723 because **OSI nests its sub-provincial
estimates inside the StatsCan provincial total**. The commission's
majority §III text on p. 28 confirms this explicitly: "Provincial and
census division population estimates are regularly produced by Statistics
Canada while municipal (census subdivision) estimates are produced by the
OSI on an annual basis" and "[t]he municipal estimates were modelled to
ensure consistency with the census divisions and provincial level
estimates produced by Statistics Canada."

In other words, when the commission says "TBF July 2024 estimate," the
provincial total component is the StatsCan figure, and the sub-provincial
component is the OSI modelled estimate. Both labels point to the same
number.

### 2.4 Are the per-ED figures derivable from the cited source?

The per-ED figures cannot come from either TBF or StatsCan directly,
because neither publishes at the proposed-ED level. The commission states
on p. 28 majority / p. 296 minority that "[b]ecause boundary drawing
requires population figures at a high level of geographic granularity,
OSI produced population estimates at the dissemination area (DA) level.
Alberta contains 6,203 dissemination areas."

The per-ED numbers therefore come from a **DA-level OSI estimate
aggregated to proposed ED boundaries**. The aggregation step (DAs to EDs)
is the load-bearing link, and the commission does not publish the DA-ED
crosswalk. Neither the DA-level file nor the aggregation rule is
reproducible from the report alone. This is a major disclosure gap; it is
what Phase 4 of the audit refers to as "shapefile not yet released" and
what this track identifies as the reproducibility deficit.

### 2.5 StatsCan 98-10-0005 (2021 Census CSD profile) provincial total

The 2021 Census of Population counted Alberta at **4,262,635**, matching
both reports' statement. This figure is consistent. No inconsistency here.

### 2.6 January 1, 2025 provincial total

The commission reports 4,960,097 on January 1, 2025 (majority p. 27;
minority p. 296). StatsCan Q4 2024 release (March 19, 2025) confirms
**Alberta January 1, 2025 population = 4,960,097**. Consistent.

Source verified: https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/daily-quotidien/250319/dq250319a-eng.htm

### 2.7 Minority table sum — the 50-person gap

The minority's p. 357–358 table of 89 EDs, when summed, equals
**4,888,773**, which is 50 higher than the stated provincial total of
4,888,723. This is not a parse error: two independent extractions
(`data/minority_2026_populations.csv` and
`data/minority_2026_populations_appendixE.csv`) both produce
4,888,773 from the PDF. The majority's p. 45 summary table sums exactly
to 4,888,723. The minority has a 50-person arithmetic gap between its
stated total and its per-ED sum.

50 out of 4,888,723 is 0.001%. The gap is too small to affect any audit
finding. It is, however, evidence that the minority's table was not a
direct aggregation of the same 4,888,723-consistent DA file the majority
used, because a consistent aggregation should produce an exact match.
Possible explanations include: (a) a rounding policy applied only to some
EDs, (b) a small handful of DAs assigned to more than one proposed ED
under the minority's different boundary set (double-counting), (c) a
minor data correction that landed in some rows but not in the stated
total, or (d) a typo. The report provides no information to distinguish
among these. The gap is listed in Phase 3 as a **minor** inconsistency.

### 2.8 Per-ED figures — Plan B cross-check consistency

The per-ED figures in both `data/majority_2026_populations.csv` and
`data/minority_2026_populations.csv` are consistent with the
4,888,723 basis (the majority to the person, the minority to within 50
persons). Plan B (analysis/reports/plan_b_cross_check.md) independently
ran the figures against the 2025 Alberta Regional Dashboard / TBF
estimates and found every audit finding stable. This Phase 2 check adds
one layer of specificity: the per-ED figures are correctly produced from
a July 2024 OSI DA-level file; the provincial total is consistent with
both StatsCan and TBF releases; the commission's text characterization
of that file as a "2021 decennial census [updated]" is the only
inconsistency that matters for the audit.

The commission's §III text is generally accurate about OSI methodology
(DA → municipality → CD → province nesting, administrative data inputs,
modelling approach). The material inconsistencies are concentrated in:
(a) the decennial-census-updated framing that implies a two-step process
the tables do not show (#1, #2); (b) the absence of a DA-level file or
DA-ED crosswalk that would make the per-ED figures reproducible (#5);
(c) the absence of a visible DIAND/CIRNAC reserve supplement (#7).

None of these are findings that the commission lied or falsified. They
are disclosure gaps. The per-ED numbers are internally consistent (to
within 50 in the minority case), and the provincial total is consistent
with both StatsCan and TBF public releases.

## Phase 4 — Materiality assessment against audit findings

### A1 — MAD asymmetry (majority 3,180 vs minority 4,707)

A1 is a **structural** finding computed from ED-level populations. Both
the majority and minority tables are already derived from the OSI July
2024 DA estimate. Correcting the Phase 1 / Phase 3 inconsistencies does
not change the inputs to A1: the tables are what they are, whether the
§III text accurately describes them or not.

If one instead re-ran A1 on 2021 Census inputs (hypothetical strict
compliance with §12(3)), both sides' populations would change
substantially (−15% on average) but the **relative** asymmetry — minority
MAD larger than majority MAD — would persist, because A1 measures
within-plan dispersion, and both plans were drawn against the same 2024
basis. Plan B in plan_b_cross_check.md confirmed this empirically.

**A1 unchanged.**

### A2 — Calgary zone gap (0.36% majority vs 12.20% minority)

A2 is also a **structural** ED-level finding. Same logic as A1: the gap
is measured between groups of EDs inside one plan, and both plans use
the same July 2024 basis. Correcting the §III characterization does not
change the tables.

Sub-CSD Calgary precision is capped by the absence of a DA-level file
release (Inconsistency #5) and by the cancellation of Calgary's civic
census (documented in Plan B). The ED-level finding stands.

**A2 unchanged.**

### A3 — s.15(2) eligibility counts

The s.15(2) test depends on (a) ED population being ≥50% below the
quota, and (b) area exceeding 20,000 km². Neither criterion interacts
with the data-source disclosure question. The four s.15(2)-eligible EDs
in each plan are determined by the same July 2024 figures the rest of
the audit uses.

**A3 unchanged.**

### B1–B6 — Partisan analysis

The partisan-bias tests use 2023 per-poll vote results, not population.
Data sources for the population audit do not affect the B-section.

**B1–B6 unchanged.**

### Summary

No audit finding changes under any plausible correction of the
inconsistencies identified here. The inconsistencies are disclosure gaps,
not arithmetic errors that propagate. This is consistent with
plan_b_cross_check.md Phase 3 conclusion that "no audit finding is
rescued by switching data sources; none is overturned."

The one observation that has independent weight is **Inconsistency #5**
(DA-level file not published). It does not change any current finding,
but it caps the audit's precision ceiling: any future sub-ED analysis
(for A2 at community level, for A3 at reserve level, for hybrid
composition at municipality level) must await public release of the OSI
DA estimates used to build the commission's tables.

## Phase 5 — Reform recommendation: §12 disclosure amendments

The inconsistencies above are not a problem with what the commission
did. They are a problem with what the commission was required to say.
Section 12 of the Electoral Boundaries Commission Act currently specifies
(in subsections 3–5) which data sources the commission shall, may, or
must supplement, but says nothing about **what the commission must
publish** so that its population figures are verifiable.

The following annotations describe disclosure obligations that a future
Act amendment should bind on any subsequent commission. Drafting counsel
should render them into statutory language; the annotations below specify
intent.

### Proposed §12(6) — Commission disclosure obligations

> **§12(6) require that** the Commission publish, as part of its report
> and in a separately-accessible machine-readable companion file, each
> of the following:
>
> **(a) require that** the Commission state, for every population figure
> used in the report, the **specific source publication** from which the
> figure is drawn, including: (i) the publication's title, (ii) its
> release date, (iii) the issuing agency, (iv) where applicable, the
> Statistics Canada catalogue number or Alberta TBF publication number,
> and (v) a URL or equivalent digital object identifier at which the
> cited publication is publicly available on the day the report is
> tabled.
>
> **(b) require that** if the population figure is produced through a
> combination of two or more sources (for example, a decennial census
> "updated" by a provincial estimate), the Commission disclose **each
> source separately, with its weight or contribution** to the figure,
> sufficient to permit independent replication of the combined figure by
> a third party who has access to the listed sources.
>
> **(c) require that** the Commission publish a **dissemination-area-to-
> electoral-division crosswalk**, listing for each proposed electoral
> division the Statistics Canada DA identifiers of every DA assigned to
> it, with DA populations totalling to the proposed ED population. The
> crosswalk shall be released simultaneously with the interim and final
> reports.
>
> **(d) require that** the Commission publish the full dissemination-
> area-level population dataset used to construct its proposed electoral
> divisions, either directly or by reference to a contemporaneously-
> published Office of Statistics and Information release that contains
> the same dataset. The release shall bear a version identifier (e.g. a
> release date) such that a later re-issuance or revision of the DA
> population file does not alter the archival record used by the
> Commission.
>
> **(e) require that** the Commission disclose, separately from other
> supplements, the specific source and magnitude of any **Indian reserve
> population supplement** incorporated under §12(3)(b) or §12(4)(d),
> including a per-reserve or per-First Nation breakdown of persons
> added, and the date of the CIRNAC / ISC file from which the supplement
> is drawn.
>
> **(f) require that** the per-ED population figures in the Commission's
> tables sum **exactly** to the stated provincial total; any deviation
> between the sum of per-ED figures and the stated provincial total
> (including rounding artefacts) be disclosed with reasons, to a
> resolution of one person.
>
> **(g) require that** the Commission characterize its data basis using
> language that accurately reflects the arithmetic of its tables. If the
> tables are derived solely from a mid-year Office of Statistics and
> Information estimate, the commission shall say so; the phrase
> "decennial census updated to…" shall only be used where the arithmetic
> visibly incorporates decennial-census figures alongside the updating
> estimate.

### Proposed §12(7) — Reproducibility standard

> **§12(7) require that** a third party, provided only with the documents
> required to be published under §12(6), be able to:
>
> **(a)** recover the Commission's provincial population total from the
> cited sources, to the person;
>
> **(b)** recover any proposed electoral division's population by summing
> DAs listed in the published crosswalk with the DA populations in the
> published dataset, to the person or to within a stated rounding
> tolerance; and
>
> **(c)** verify that each §15(2) electoral division meets the statutory
> criteria of §15(2)(a)–(c) from the published geospatial boundary file
> and population data.

Rationale: clauses (a)–(g) close every gap identified in Phase 3. Clause
(a) closes #3 (incomplete source attribution). Clauses (b) and (g) close
#1 and #2 (decennial-census-updated framing that does not match the
tables). Clauses (c) and (d) close #5 (DA file not published; DA-ED
crosswalk missing). Clause (e) closes #7 (reserve supplement not visibly
disclosed). Clause (f) closes #4 (minority 50-person sum gap). Clause
§12(7) formalizes the reproducibility expectation that the audit sought
to apply to the present report and was unable to meet at the DA level.

Proposed §12(6) and §12(7) are disclosure-only amendments. They do not
alter the substantive standard under §12(3)–(5) for which data sources
the commission may use. They do not require the commission to use the
decennial census over the TBF estimate or vice versa. They only require
that whatever source is used be disclosed in enough detail for
verification.

## Summary for the parent session

Track O verified the commission's population-data disclosures against
their cited and implied sources. The commission's provincial total of
4,888,723 is consistent with both StatsCan Table 17-10-0009 (July 1, 2024
Alberta quarterly estimate) and the Alberta TBF Annual Population Report
2023–2024. The per-ED tables are internally consistent with that total
(majority: exact; minority: 50-person gap, immaterial). The material
disclosure issue is the §III characterization of the basis as "2021
decennial census updated to July 1, 2024 estimate," which is inconsistent
with the tables' direct use of the July 2024 OSI estimate without a
visible 2021 census component. No audit finding (A1, A2, A3, B1–B6)
changes under any plausible correction of the inconsistencies. The
single upstream gap that caps audit precision is the absence of a
published DA-level file and DA-ED crosswalk, which prevents independent
sub-ED reconstruction.
