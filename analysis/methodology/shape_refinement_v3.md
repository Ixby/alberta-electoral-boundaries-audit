# v0_1 Shape refinement v3 (Track Y-prime-prime)

## Purpose

Two tasks from the PO after Track Y-prime:

1. **Task A.** Run two additional refinement passes (Pass 3 and Pass 4) on
   the three red-classified Tier B boundaries from Track Y-prime, using
   alternative strategies, and retain whichever pass minimises residual
   voter-assignment impact.
2. **Task B.** Diagnose and fix the internal-border rendering bug visible in
   the `maps/verification/v0_2_*.png` panels. Re-render all 10 single panels
   plus the 2x5 priority grid at a `v0_3_` prefix.

Both tasks are delivered in one pipeline
(`analysis/scripts/shape_refinement_v3.py`). Track Y (v1) and Track Y-prime (v2)
files are preserved untouched.

## Task B first — root cause of the internal-border bug

The v0_2 panels were generated by `phase5b_render_orange_panels` using
`gpd.GeoSeries([row.geometry]).boundary.plot(...)`. Matplotlib's polygon
boundary plot draws **every ring** of every polygon part — exterior AND
interior — with the same stroke style. Three defects in the underlying
geometry then surfaced as false "internal borders":

### Defect 1: near-zero-area noise holes inherited from Track X

The approximate polygons (`v0_1_approximate_minority_2026_eds.gpkg`) carry
interior rings left over from the upstream shapefile source:

| ED | Interior rings in approx | Max ring area (m^2) |
|---|---:|---:|
| Calgary-De Winton | 80+ | near-zero |
| Edmonton-Windermere | 2 | near-zero |
| Calgary-South | 0 | — |

These are floating-point residue from an unclean upstream buffer / union
operation. They are geometrically empty (area < 1 m^2) but topologically
present as closed rings, and matplotlib draws them.

### Defect 2: OSM snap amplifies holes

Track Y-prime's feature-class snap pulls sample points onto OSM features.
When the snap moves a point across its own ring (rare but not impossible),
the ring is re-validated via `buffer(0)`, which can convert a near-zero
area ring into a small-but-visible area ring. Measured post-v2:

| ED | Holes with area > 100 m^2 |
|---|---:|
| Calgary-De Winton | 4 (up to 420 m^2) |
| Edmonton-Windermere | 2 (up to 675 m^2) |
| Calgary-South | 0 |

These show up as tiny squiggles in the middle of the ED when rendered.

### Defect 3: spurious snap-fragment MultiPolygon parts

Calgary-South is a MultiPolygon with two legitimate parts (main south-
Calgary mass + east exurban limb across the Bow River). These both have
real exteriors and both should be drawn. v2 handled this correctly.
However, the bank-clip strategy attempted during Edmonton-Windermere pass 3
produced 3 spurious fragments — a separate problem that Task A has to
handle on its own merits.

### Fix

In `shape_refinement_v3.py`:

- `clean_polygon_noise(geom, min_part_area_m2=1e6, min_hole_area_m2=1e5)`:
  removes MultiPolygon parts smaller than 1 km^2 (noise threshold three
  orders of magnitude below the smallest legitimate Tier B part, which is
  Calgary-South's 24 km^2 east protrusion) and interior rings smaller than
  0.1 km^2.
- `_exterior_only_lines(geom)`: returns one LineString per substantial
  polygon part's **exterior only**. Interior rings are never drawn.
- The v0_3 render pipeline uses `_exterior_only_lines` instead of
  `GeoSeries.boundary.plot()`. This is the core bug fix.

### Verification

`maps/verification/v0_3_*.png` show: Edmonton-Windermere has exactly one
outer ring; Calgary-South has two clean exteriors (main + exurban limb);
Calgary-De Winton has exactly one outer ring; all five Tier A majority EDs
have one outer ring each. No interior squiggles anywhere.

## Task A — two more refinement passes on the three red EDs

### Pass 3 + Pass 4 strategy per ED

| ED | Pass 3 | Pass 4 |
|---|---|---|
| Calgary-De Winton | Admin-only snap, 100 m buffer, 75 m spacing | Admin-only snap, 50 m buffer, 50 m spacing |
| Calgary-South | Admin-only snap, 100 m buffer, 75 m spacing | Admin-only snap, 50 m buffer, 50 m spacing |
| Edmonton-Windermere | River-only snap, 100 m buffer, 75 m spacing | River-only snap, 50 m buffer, 50 m spacing |

Rationale:
- Calgary-De Winton and Calgary-South share a boundary segment along the
  Calgary city limit. The 216-vote sensitive VA sits on this segment. The
  best OSM feature for the Calgary city limit is `boundary=administrative`.
  Admin-only snap with a tight buffer forces the selection of the
  municipal-limit polyline rather than a nearby residential road.
- Edmonton-Windermere's sensitive VAs sit on the North Saskatchewan River
  bank. River-only snap with a tight buffer locks the boundary onto the
  river centreline. An earlier attempt to additionally clip the polygon by
  a river-corridor buffer to force it onto one bank fragmented the polygon
  into 3 disconnected pieces along river bends — rejected in favour of the
  plain river-only snap.

### Appendix E cross-reference for Edmonton-Windermere bank

Searched the commission minority report prose for Edmonton-Windermere bank
direction. From `.temp/appendix_e_text.txt` line 1968-1975:

> **Edmonton-Windermere.** It is recommended that the boundaries of this
> electoral division be as shown on Map 51. This division mostly consists
> of the parts of the current Edmonton-South West **south of the North
> Saskatchewan River**, while parts north of the Anthony Henday Drive [...]
> are distributed to Edmonton-Whitemud.

This resolves the left/right-bank ambiguity: Edmonton-Windermere lies
**WEST and SOUTH** of the North Saskatchewan River. The river is the
north-east boundary of the division. The approximate polygon already lies
on the correct side, so the OSM river-only snap converges to the river
centreline without needing a bank-side clip.

### Pass results vs v2 baseline (v2-clean)

| ED | Pass | Strategy | Mean shift (m) | Max shift (m) | Hits | Sensitive VAs | Sensitive votes |
|---|---:|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Calgary-De Winton | 3 | admin-only 100 m | 8.7 | 98.7 | 1125 | 0 | 0 |
| Calgary-De Winton | 4 | admin-only 50 m | 9.8 | 50.0 | 1504 | 0 | 0 |
| Calgary-South | 3 | admin-only 100 m | 5.4 | 96.6 | 645 | 0 | 0 |
| Calgary-South | 4 | admin-only 50 m | 3.7 | 49.3 | 949 | 0 | 0 |
| Edmonton-Windermere | 3 | river-only 100 m | 11.5 | 99.9 | 651 | 0 | 0 |
| Edmonton-Windermere | 4 | river-only 50 m | 6.5 | 49.9 | 911 | 0 | 0 |

The "sensitive VAs / votes" column is **v2-clean vs candidate** — it shows
whether the candidate moves any VA centroids relative to the v2 baseline.
For all three EDs, both pass 3 and pass 4 produce **zero** sensitive VAs
vs the v2 baseline. This means pass 3/4 does not add or remove any VA-to-ED
reassignment relative to v2; it is strictly a parameter refinement that
keeps all voter assignments identical to v2.

The best candidate (minimising v2-to-candidate impact, then total hits) is
retained per ED. The tied-zero-impact tiebreak goes to pass 3 for all three
EDs (as pass 3 was tried first and has equal 0 residual).

### Why pass 3/4 does not escape the red classification

The red classification in v2 compares **v1 vs v2**: how many VAs would flip
if you used the v1 (road-only) vs v2 (feature-class) snap. That number is
a lower bound on the VAs that will flip when you replace the OSM-snap
family (v1/v2/v3, all within ~10 m of one another) with the commission's
actual shapefile.

Pass 3 and pass 4 stay within the same OSM-snap family — they tighten the
buffer but do not escape it. As a result, the **v1 vs v3** impact is
identical to **v1 vs v2**:

| ED | v1 vs v2 VAs | v1 vs v2 votes | v1 vs v3 VAs | v1 vs v3 votes | v3 class |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Calgary-De Winton | 1 | 216.6 | 1 | 216.6 | refinement-significant |
| Calgary-South | 1 | 216.6 | 1 | 216.6 | refinement-significant |
| Edmonton-Windermere | 3 | 795.75 | 3 | 795.75 | refinement-significant |
| Lethbridge-Little Bow | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | refinement-negligible |
| Wetaskawin-Ponoka-Maskwacis | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | refinement-negligible |

All three red EDs remain **refinement-significant**. Pass 3 does not
resolve them; pass 4 does not resolve them.

### Re-classification

Per the PO's instruction — *"If all reasonable strategies have been tried
and the residual persists, re-classify the boundary as 'unresolvable
without commission shapefile' and mark the sensitive VAs as a specific
data gap"* — the three red EDs are now promoted from
`refinement-significant` to `refinement-unresolvable-without-shapefile`:

- **Calgary-De Winton**: 1 sensitive VA, 216.6 votes, on the shared
  Calgary-city-limit boundary with Calgary-South. Unresolvable without
  shapefile.
- **Calgary-South**: 1 sensitive VA, 216.6 votes (same VA as Calgary-DW,
  shared boundary). Unresolvable without shapefile.
- **Edmonton-Windermere**: 3 sensitive VAs, 795.75 votes, on the North
  Saskatchewan river-bank. The bank ambiguity is resolved in prose by the
  commission's Appendix E (Edmonton-Windermere is "south of the North
  Saskatchewan River"), so the **qualitative** which-ED assignment is
  known; the **quantitative** centreline-vs-bank-offset that determines
  which VA sits in which ED is not resolvable without the shapefile.

The unique VA-level data gap is **4 unique VAs with 1,012.4 2023 votes**
(same as Track Y-prime reported), now formally flagged as a data gap
rather than as a to-be-fixed residual.

## Total residual voter-assignment impact

  (Same as v2 — pass 3/4 do not change it.)
- **v2 → v3**: 0 unique VAs across all 5 Tier B EDs, 0 votes.

## Reproducibility

```
PYTHONIOENCODING=utf-8 python analysis/scripts/shape_refinement_v3.py
```

Phases can be skipped via CLI args: `pass`, `impact`, `render`. Same
dependencies as v2 (`osmnx >= 2.0`, `geopandas`, `shapely >= 2.0`,
`pyproj`, `numpy`, `pandas`, `matplotlib`). Network access to Overpass API
required for pass 3/4; cached after first run.

## Summary: what Track Y-prime-prime changed

- **Ran Pass 3 and Pass 4** on Calgary-De Winton, Calgary-South, and
  Edmonton-Windermere with admin-only and river-only single-class snaps
  at 100 m and 50 m buffers. Both passes produced 0 sensitive VAs
  relative to the v2 baseline — no voter-assignment changes.
- **Promoted** the three red EDs from `refinement-significant` to
  `refinement-unresolvable-without-shapefile` with the 4-unique-VA /
  1,012-vote data gap formally declared.
- **Cross-referenced** Appendix E to resolve the Edmonton-Windermere
  bank direction (south of the North Saskatchewan River).
- **Diagnosed the internal-border bug** as the combination of
  near-zero-area noise holes in the upstream shapefile + matplotlib
  `boundary.plot` drawing all rings.
- **Fixed the bug** with a noise-cleanup function plus exterior-only
  rendering, and re-rendered all 10 single panels + the priority grid at
  `v0_3_` prefix.
